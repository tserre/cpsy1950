# CPSY 1950 — Course Outline (Spring 2026)

## Core Format

- **T/Th, 80 minutes each**
- **Tuesday:** Lecture introducing the week's theme (conceptual, figure-first)
- **Thursday:** Lightning mini-conference on the same theme
  - ~15 lightning talks (3 minutes each)
  - Synthesis discussion/activity

## Group & Presentation Plan (for up to 103 students)

- Lightning talks are prepared in small groups (typically 3, occasionally 4)
- Groups are not fixed: students form new groups for each lightning week (rotating teams)
- **Each student presents exactly 3 lightning talks** over the semester (Weeks 3-9)
- Posters are reserved for student projects (after spring break)
- **Equity:** Presentation turns tracked at individual student level

**Goal:** Put everyone on a leveled playing field for Week 3. Complete the Required Core first (mandatory for all), then the conditional tracks that match your background. You do not need to do every track.

Expected time: ~90 min total

- **Marr's Levels of Analysis (video):**

- **Coarse brain organization (video; focus on gross organization + functions):**
  MIT OCW 9.13 (Spring 2019) Lecture 2: "Neuroanatomy"
  - Viewing guidance: Watch for the high-level tour of major brain divisions/structures and what they do. Pay particular attention to concepts of receptive fields, neural tuning, and cortical maps.

## Course Mechanics Summary

### Lightning Talks (Weeks 3-9)
- Small groups (typically 3, occasionally 4 students)
- 3-minute presentation
- Rotating teams each week
- ~15 groups per session

### Project Posters (Weeks 11-12)
- Individual or small group projects
- Two poster sessions (17 posters each)
- Studio time provided in Week 11

### Bootcamp (Week 2)
- Asynchronous learning
- Required core + conditional tracks based on background
- Bootcamp checkpoint: Crick (1989) reading + response
